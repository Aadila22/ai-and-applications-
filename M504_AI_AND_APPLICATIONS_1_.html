H&M Store Network Analysis and Operational Analysis¶

Business Context¶

So I am chosen as the Data Science Consultant of H&M where my responsibility is to analysis the store network and operational effectiveness of H&M. The aim of this project it to derive analysis to faciliate decision-making on where to open a store, the type of store in terms of category and the hours of operation across the cities and countries listed. By using the store-level data, I hope to help H&M to do efficient store operations and manage resources, to seek for better customer engagement and to look for better plans of expansion.

Objective¶

Tilt your head and take a close look at your H&M store network and operations patterns and insights that help you perform well and keep optimizing your opening hours, expansion strategies, and resource planning.

City by city and country by country map of stores

  • Categorize stores by type

  • Analyze opening hours by day

  • Calculate average open hours

  • Visualize store locations and clusters

  • Suggest operational improvements

Data Source¶

For this project we will be using dataset from Kaggle: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/shivamb/hm-stores-dataset

Business Questions¶

The following questions have been formulated in order to work on this project:

  1. Where are the most H&M stores located?

  2. Where are the different store types (Flagship, Red, Blue) located in countries and cities?

  3. What are the general weekday vs weekend hours for an H&M?

  4. Which types of stores have stores with the longest average daily hours open, and how does this compare to the location of the store?

  5. What is the spread of H&M stores geographically, and are there hot-spots / cold-spots that could tell us about the expansion?

plt.show()

Strength of Dataset analysis¶

Following are the strength of sucessfully performed data analysis:

  • Dataset offerd a wide range of storage level details enables to identify the location trends and operational patterns.

  • The dataset is clean with no missing values in the crucial categories keeping the analysis consistant.

  • Categorical variables are diversed that allowed proper analysis based on specific factor.

Drawbacks of the analysis and dataset¶

Considering the analysis process, the area of focus I was given has certain constraints because of the dataset. They are:

  • Dataset shows store locations and opening hours but doesn’t include sales or customer visit numbers, so we can’t tell which stores perform best or are busiest.

  • It’s just a snapshot in time, so we can’t see how the store network has grown or changed over the years.

  • Important details like staff levels, inventory, or promotions aren’t included, which limits understanding of day-to-day store operations.

Conclusion¶

Insight:

The business questions were effectively addressed and thus the following insights can be concluded :

  • Most stores fall under the Red class, showing it's H&M’s most common and flexible format.

  • Moscow and Warsaw have the highest store counts — these cities are clearly strong markets.

  • Stores are generally open longer on weekends, especially Fridays and Saturdays.

  • Lisbon stood out for having stores with the longest working hours, especially in the Red category.

  • There's potential for expansion in cities like Gdansk and Krakow where the brand’s presence is still small.

  • Focus on expansion of stores in the most demanded areas.

  • Improve the marketing methods in the low performed stores , or else relocate to most promising areas.

  • Adjust the opening hours based on customer local habits and preferences of the customer in diffrent cities.

  • decision making regarding opening of new stores, on the based on store type stay open for longer hours.
